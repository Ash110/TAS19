Radio's and everything about them seem very 1960' and rather boring dont they? Well we are going to change that, by using radio frequency to control bots! Take part in the TAS workshop bring radio tech back into the 21th century.

In this interesting event, the student will control his bot/car by a remote in his hand. This teaches the student about Radio Frequency communication, Keypad interfacing, Encoders, Decoders, Motors and Motor drivers. Direction command to the bot is given using a keypad. This signal is sent to the RF emitter through an encoder HT12E which converts parallel data to serial. RF receiver receives the serial data sent by the emitter and decodes it using HT12D. This decoded parallel dataset is used as commands to acuate the motor. IC L293D is used as a motor driver in this circuit.

Rules :
  1. Teams of upto 4.
  2. No prerequisites; all materials will be supplied to you.
  3. The morning session will be from 9-12, and the afternoon session from 12:30-3:30
